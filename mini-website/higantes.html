History and Significance

The Higantes Festival is celebrated every November 23 in Angono, Rizal, the “Art Capital of the Philippines.” It pays tribute to San Clemente, the town’s patron saint, with a procession of colorful giant papier-mâché effigies locally called higantes.

The festival traces its roots to the Spanish colonial period, when Angono residents used giant figures as a form of protest against Spanish landlords. Today, it has transformed into a joyful celebration of art, creativity, and community pride, attracting both local and international visitors.

Festival Highlights

  • Parade of giant papier-mâché figures or higantes
  • Fluvial procession in honor of San Clemente
  • Street dancing and cultural performances
  • Art exhibits showcasing Angono’s creativity

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Higantes Festival celebrated?

The festival is held annually on November 23 in Angono, Rizal.

What are Higantes made of?

They are made of papier-mâché, bamboo, and other lightweight materials, painted in vibrant colors.

Can tourists join the festivities?

Yes! Visitors are welcome to join the street celebrations and view the Higantes parade.
