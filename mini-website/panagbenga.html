History and Significance

The Panagbenga Festival, meaning “season of blooming,” is held every February in Baguio City. First celebrated in 1995, it was created to boost the city’s economy after the 1990 Luzon earthquake. Since then, it has grown into one of the country’s most iconic festivals, attracting millions of visitors from around the world.

Panagbenga highlights the rich culture of the Cordillera region through street dancing inspired by the movements of flowers and nature. Its main attraction is the Grand Float Parade, featuring spectacular floats covered entirely in vibrant flowers. The festival also emphasizes community involvement, environmental awareness, and the creative spirit of the people of Baguio.

Festival Highlights

  • Grand Float Parade featuring flower-covered floats
  • Street dancing inspired by flowers and Cordillera traditions
  • Market encounters and community trade fairs
  • Exhibitions, concerts, and cultural showcases

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Panagbenga Festival celebrated?

The festival is held every February in Baguio City.

What does "Panagbenga" mean?

It means "season of blooming," symbolizing Baguio’s flowers and culture.

What is the highlight of Panagbenga?

The Grand Float Parade, where floats are decorated with thousands of fresh flowers, is the most awaited event.
