History and Significance

The Kadayawan Festival is held every August in Davao City as a celebration of life, culture, and thanksgiving for the bountiful harvest of fruits, flowers, and other natural resources. The term “Kadayawan” comes from the Mandaya word “madayaw,” which means good, valuable, or beautiful.

Rooted in the traditions of the indigenous tribes of Davao, the festival highlights the city’s rich cultural diversity and unity. It has grown into one of the country’s grandest festivals, attracting thousands of tourists who come to witness its colorful street dances, floral floats, and tribal showcases.

Festival Highlights

  • Street dancing competition inspired by tribal rituals
  • Floral float parade showcasing Davao’s harvest
  • Exhibitions of indigenous crafts, music, and traditions
  • Fruit and flower displays celebrating Davao’s abundance

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Kadayawan Festival celebrated?

The festival is held every third week of August in Davao City.

What does "Kadayawan" mean?

"Kadayawan" is derived from the Mandaya word “madayaw,” which means good, beautiful, or valuable.

Can tourists join the celebrations?

Yes! The festival welcomes both locals and tourists to join the parades, concerts, and cultural showcases.
