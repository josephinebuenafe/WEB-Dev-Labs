History and Significance

The Kaamulan Festival is celebrated every March in Malaybalay City, Bukidnon. It is a gathering of the province’s seven hill tribes: Bukidnon, Higaonon, Talaandig, Manobo, Matigsalug, Tigwahanon, and Umayamnon. The word “Kaamulan” comes from the Binukid word meaning “to gather.”

The festival showcases the cultural heritage, traditions, and unity of the indigenous peoples of Bukidnon. It is one of the few authentic ethnic festivals in the Philippines, featuring tribal rituals, music, dances, and crafts that highlight the identity and pride of the people of Bukidnon.

Festival Highlights

  • Ethnic street dancing featuring Bukidnon’s seven tribes
  • Traditional rituals such as weddings and healing ceremonies
  • Exhibitions of indigenous crafts, music, and dances
  • Horse fights, trade fairs, and agricultural shows
