History and Significance

The Dinagyang Festival is one of the most famous religious and cultural festivals in the Philippines. Held every fourth Sunday of January in Iloilo City, it honors the Santo Niño (Child Jesus) and commemorates the arrival of Malay settlers and the peaceful pact with the native Ati people.

The word "Dinagyang" comes from the Hiligaynon word meaning “merrymaking.” Known for its spectacular choreography, extravagant costumes, and powerful drumbeats, Dinagyang reflects Iloilo’s devotion, artistry, and community pride. Over the years, it has gained international recognition as a world-class festival of faith and culture.

Festival Highlights

  • Spectacular Ati-Ati street dancing competitions
  • Elaborate costumes and synchronized choreography
  • Lively drumbeats and traditional chants
  • Fluvial procession and religious activities

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Dinagyang Festival celebrated?

It is celebrated every fourth Sunday of January in Iloilo City.

What does “Dinagyang” mean?

It means “merrymaking” in the Hiligaynon language, reflecting the joyful spirit of the festival.

Can tourists join the activities?

Yes! Visitors can watch performances, explore food fairs, and enjoy the festive atmosphere.
