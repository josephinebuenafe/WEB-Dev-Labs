History and Significance

The MassKara Festival, held every October in Bacolod City, is famously known as the “Festival of Smiles.” It began in 1980 during a period of crisis when the sugar industry, the city’s main livelihood, suffered a huge downfall. At the same time, tragedy struck with the sinking of the MV Don Juan, which took many lives from Negros. In response, local leaders and artists created a festival that would uplift the spirits of the people.

The highlight of MassKara is the use of colorful, smiling masks that symbolize the resilience and positivity of Bacolodnons despite hardships. Dancers in elaborate costumes fill the streets, accompanied by upbeat music, while the city bursts with parades, food festivals, and nightly concerts.

Today, the MassKara Festival has grown into one of the Philippines’ grandest celebrations, drawing tourists worldwide to experience the unique mix of creativity, joy, and Bacolod’s warm hospitality.

Festival Highlights

  • Street dancing competition with colorful masks and costumes
  • Concerts, food fairs, and trade expos showcasing Bacolod’s culture
  • Electric MassKara parade with illuminated floats and dancers
  • Cultural shows and activities celebrating Bacolod’s resilience and joy

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the MassKara Festival celebrated?

The festival is held annually every October in Bacolod City.

Why do people wear masks with smiles?

The smiling masks symbolize the resilience and optimism of Bacolodnons despite hardships.

Can visitors join the street dancing?

Yes, tourists are welcome to enjoy and even join the festive atmosphere during the parades and concerts.
