History and Significance

The Pintados Festival is held annually in Tacloban City, Leyte, usually every June, as a tribute to Señor Santo Niño. The word “Pintados” means “painted,” referring to the ancient Visayan warriors who adorned their bodies with elaborate tattoos symbolizing bravery, rank, and accomplishments in battle.

Today, the festival blends religious devotion with cultural pride, featuring street dancing, traditional music, and vibrant parades. Participants paint their bodies with intricate patterns, mimicking the tattoos of their ancestors. The Pintados Festival not only preserves pre-colonial traditions but also celebrates faith, unity, and resilience among the people of Leyte.

Festival Highlights

  • Street dancing with warriors painted in colorful patterns
  • Grand parades honoring Señor Santo Niño
  • Cultural performances showcasing pre-colonial traditions
  • Exhibits, food fairs, and local music shows

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Pintados Festival celebrated?

The Pintados Festival is usually held in June in Tacloban City, Leyte.

What does “Pintados” mean?

It means “painted,” referring to the tattooed Visayan warriors of pre-colonial times.

Can tourists participate in the street parade?

Yes, visitors are welcome to join the festivities and experience Leyte’s rich culture.
