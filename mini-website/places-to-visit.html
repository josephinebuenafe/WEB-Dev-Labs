Festival Map of the Philippines

Festivals are spread across Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao, showcasing the Philippines’ cultural diversity. Use this map to locate the major festivals and plan your travel route. Each marker represents a city or town where one of the 15 featured festivals is celebrated.

Notable Festival Locations

Cebu City – Sinulog Festival

A vibrant celebration in honor of the Sto. Niño, featuring street dances, fluvial processions, and grand parades.

Baguio City – Panagbenga Festival

Known as the Flower Festival, this event highlights floats decorated with fresh blooms and street performances that attract thousands of visitors.

Bacolod City – MassKara Festival

Famous for its colorful masks and lively street parties, Bacolod is known as the “City of Smiles.”

Kalibo, Aklan – Ati-Atihan Festival

A historic and spiritual festival where locals paint their faces black and dance in honor of the Santo Niño.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I use the festival map?

The map highlights the locations of the 15 festivals across the Philippines. You can zoom in, click on the area, and plan your route from nearby airports or bus stations.

Are these festival locations tourist-friendly?

Yes, most festival areas are major cities or towns with hotels, restaurants, and transport options. Some rural festivals may require extra planning for travel and lodging.

Can I attend multiple festivals in one trip?

It’s possible if you plan carefully, since some festivals happen in the same month. For example, Sinulog, Dinagyang, and Ati-Atihan are all in January.

Do I need to hire a guide?

Not necessarily. Most festivals are easy to navigate, but hiring a local guide can give you insider tips, history, and access to hidden spots.
