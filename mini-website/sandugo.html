History and Significance

The Sandugo Festival, celebrated every July in Tagbilaran City, Bohol, commemorates the historic blood compact or “Sandugo” between Datu Sikatuna, a native chieftain, and Miguel López de Legazpi, a Spanish explorer, in 1565. This blood compact symbolized peace, friendship, and alliance between the Filipinos and the Spaniards.

Today, the Sandugo Festival is more than a reenactment of history—it is a vibrant showcase of Boholano culture, artistry, and unity. The festival features grand street dancing competitions, colorful parades, cultural shows, and trade fairs. It attracts thousands of visitors yearly, serving as a celebration of Bohol’s rich traditions and its people’s hospitality.

Festival Highlights

  • Reenactment of the 1565 Blood Compact between Datu Sikatuna and Miguel López de Legazpi
  • Street dancing competitions with vibrant costumes
  • Cultural showcases featuring Boholano heritage
  • Trade fairs, concerts, and food festivals

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Sandugo Festival celebrated?

The festival is held every July in Tagbilaran City, Bohol.

What does “Sandugo” mean?

“Sandugo” means “one blood,” symbolizing the bond of friendship created through the blood compact of 1565.

Can tourists join the festival activities?

Yes, tourists are welcome to join parades, concerts, and other cultural showcases during the celebration.
