History and Significance

The Giant Lantern Festival, also known as Ligligan Parul, is held annually in December in San Fernando, Pampanga—the “Christmas Capital of the Philippines.” It began as a small tradition of simple lantern-making during the Spanish colonial period but has since evolved into a grand competition showcasing massive, intricate lanterns that light up the night sky.

These giant lanterns, some reaching up to 20 feet in diameter, are made of steel frames, colored plastics, and thousands of light bulbs controlled by synchronized switches. The festival is not just a contest but a celebration of creativity, craftsmanship, and the Kapampangan spirit of unity and resilience.

Festival Highlights

  • Competition of giant lanterns crafted by different barangays
  • Dazzling lights and synchronized music displays
  • Parades and cultural presentations
  • Food stalls and Christmas bazaars

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Giant Lantern Festival held?

The festival is held every December, usually the Saturday before Christmas Eve.

Why is San Fernando called the "Christmas Capital of the Philippines"?

Because of its world-famous Giant Lantern Festival, showcasing dazzling lanterns symbolizing Filipino Christmas spirit.

Can tourists watch the lantern competition?

Yes! The festival is open to the public and draws both local and international visitors every year.
