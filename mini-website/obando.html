History and Significance

The Obando Fertility Rites Festival, held every May in Obando, Bulacan, is one of the Philippines’ most unique celebrations. It combines Catholic devotion with pre-colonial fertility rituals, where devotees dance to pray for children, good harvests, and even a suitable life partner.

The festival honors three patron saints: St. Paschal Baylon, St. Clare of Assisi, and Our Lady of Salambao. Couples desiring to have children join the ritual dance known as “Sayaw sa Obando” as a form of supplication. Farmers also pray for prosperity in their fields, while singles participate in hopes of finding their true love.

Beyond its spiritual meaning, the Obando Festival is also a vibrant display of Filipino faith, music, and community spirit. Street dances, colorful processions, and folk traditions come together, attracting pilgrims, tourists, and locals alike.

Festival Highlights

  • Traditional “Sayaw sa Obando” ritual dance for fertility and blessings
  • Colorful processions honoring three patron saints
  • Lively street parades with music and folk dances
  • Pilgrimage of devotees seeking children, good harvests, or a life partner

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Obando Fertility Rites Festival celebrated?

It is held annually every May 17–19 in Obando, Bulacan.

What is the purpose of the ritual dance?

The “Sayaw sa Obando” is performed to ask for children, good harvests, or even a suitable life partner.

Can tourists join the dance?

Yes, visitors are encouraged to participate in the dances along with devotees during the procession.
