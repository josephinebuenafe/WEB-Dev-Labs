History and Significance

The Pahiyas Festival is celebrated every May 15 in Lucban, Quezon, in honor of San Isidro Labrador, the patron saint of farmers. It is one of the most colorful harvest festivals in the Philippines, showcasing the creativity and gratitude of the townspeople.

During the festival, houses are decorated with agricultural products such as fruits, vegetables, rice, and the famous kipingâ€”brightly colored rice wafers crafted into elaborate shapes. What began as a simple form of thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest has become a national attraction that draws visitors from across the country and abroad.

Festival Highlights

  • Houses decorated with vegetables, fruits, and kiping rice wafers
  • Religious procession honoring San Isidro Labrador
  • Street performances, music, and parades
  • Local delicacies and community trade fairs

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Pahiyas Festival celebrated?

It is held every May 15 in Lucban, Quezon.

What is "kiping"?

Kiping is a colorful rice wafer used as decoration during the festival, often shaped into flowers and patterns.

What makes Pahiyas unique?

The festival is famous for its houses lavishly decorated with agricultural products, making the whole town look like an art gallery.
